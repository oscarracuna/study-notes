Dumpster diving
	 - Dumpster divers look through trash or recycling containers for valuable paperwork, such those containing PII.
	 - Paperwork should be shredded or incinerated.

Tailgating
	- It's the practice of following closely behind someone else without using credentials.

Prepending
	- It's a social engineering tactic. The attacker sets up a scenario in which they'd have a better chance of getting someone to release information

Shoulder surfing
	- It's the practice of peering over a person's should to discover information.

Spam
	- Spam is unwanted emails from any source for any reason.

Smishing
	- Smising is like phishing, but instead of sending emails it sends text messages to victims.

Spear phishing
	- Spear phishing is a type of phishing that targets employees in the same organization.

URL Hijacking or Typo Squatting
	- Uses a similar domain name to redirect traffic.

Whaling
	- It's a type of phishing that targets high-level executives, such as chief financial officers (CFOs) or chief executive officers (CEOs).

Rootkit
	- A rootkit typically runs hidden processes and it commonly attempts to connect to computers via the internet.

Rainbow table attack
	- Attempts to discover the password from the hash. A rainbow table are databases of precomputed hashes matched up with the corresponding passwords.

Spraying attack
	- Attempts to bypass an account lockout policy looping through a list of user accounts.
